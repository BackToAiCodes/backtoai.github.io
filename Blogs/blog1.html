FIFA partner with United Nations agencies to run social campaigns during World Cup

  • John Doe
  • Jan 1, 2022

As the clock approaches the start of the FIFA World Cup 2022TM, the world's attention is increasingly focused on the world's largest celebration of its most popular sport. Naturally, the focus will be on football, but FIFA recognises that our sport should use the unique platform that our competition provides to make a positive impact on society.

In keeping with the approach taken in previous tournaments, FIFA, in collaboration with three United Nations agencies, will run a variety of campaigns during the FIFA World Cup Qatar 2022TM to send messages promoting health, unity, and non-discrimination to billions of global viewers via multiple channels. These campaigns were developed in response to feedback from our member associations, who, like FIFA, are dedicated to using the power of football to effect positive change around the world.

Each round of matches will be accompanied by its own dedicated marketing aimed at increasing reach and effect. These will be operated in collaboration with FIFA's United Nations partners, including the United Nations Educational, Scientific, and Cultural Organization (UNESCO), the World Food Programme (WFP), and the World Health Organization (WHO)

Opporunity to share the message

Messaging opportunities will be provided to the participating teams via the team captains' armbands during matches, with supporting content being displayed on LED screens around the pitch, on giant screens and on flags in the stadiums, together with additional amplification on FIFAâ€™s digital platforms and through media, stakeholders, and other public activities.

FIFA World Cup Qatar 2022.

The FIFA World Cup Qatar 2022TM begins on Sunday, November 20th, with the host nation taking against Ecuador at Al Bayt Stadium. A total of 64 matches will be played, with the final taking place on Sunday, December 18, 2022, at Lusail Stadium. By banding together behind these projects, FIFA and its 211 member associations will demonstrate to the world that, despite our differences, football has the potential to bring people together for the common good.

The United Nations is an intergovernmental organisation whose declared goals are to ensure international peace and security, foster cordial relations among nations, accomplish international collaboration, and serve as a focal point for nations' actions to be coordinated.
