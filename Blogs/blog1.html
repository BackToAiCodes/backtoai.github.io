Creating Images from Text

  • John Doe
  • Jan 1, 2022

DALLE is a 12-billion parameter version of GPT-3 that was trained on a dataset of text-image pairs to produce images from text descriptions. We discovered that it has a wide range of capabilities, including the ability to anthropomorphize animals and objects, combine unrelated concepts in realistic ways, generate text, and make alterations to existing images.

Overview

DALLE is a language model that transforms. It receives both the text and the image as a single stream of data containing up to 1280 tokens and is trained to create all of the tokens sequentially using maximum likelihood. This training procedure allows DALL·E to not only generate an image from scratch, but also to regenerate any rectangular region of an existing image that extends to the bottom-right corner, in a way that is consistent with the text prompt.

We realise that work utilising generative models has the potential to have far-reaching societal implications. We intend to investigate how models like DALLE relate to social issues such as the economic impact on specific work processes and professions, the possibility for bias in model outputs, and the longer-term ethical challenges posed by this technology in the future.

Capabilities

We discover that DALLE can generate credible visuals for a wide range of phrases that investigate the compositional structure of language. In the following part, we demonstrate this through a series of interactive visualisations. Apart from the thumbnails and independent images that appear outside the visuals, the samples shown for each caption in the visuals are obtained by taking the top 32 of 512 after reranking using CLIP.

”Wildflowers, grassy field, autumn rhythm, watercolor“
