A fine arts competition was won by an AI artist, and traditional artists are outraged.

  • John Doe
  • Jan 1, 2022

AI art generators have had a huge impact on digital art and the development of new digital artworks in recent months. You can simply tell the current generation of tools, such as DALL-E 2, MidJourney, and Stable Diffusion, what you want them to create, whether it's Sponge Bob Square Pants mingling with Godzilla, a bizarre future environment, or a realistic still life in the style of a Renaissance master. These tools are a terrific creative outlet for those who lack the programming skills and computational power to design their own unique algorithms and just wish to get into AI art and start generating their own AI-generated art..

All of this has spurred heated philosophical debate about the future of photographers, designers, and artists, with some concerned that these technology may someday supersede human creators. To add fuel to the fire, someone just won first place in a fine art competition using an AI-generated artwork, and traditional painters are outraged.

The use of an AI art generation in an art competition has infuriated both artists and non-artists. In response to the Colorado State Fair's tweet, one person wrote: "That is ridiculous. diminishes the status of the entire art competition to the point of absurdity" "Wow, someone can buy software, change a prompt, and call the outcome art. I'll make it a point to do it the following year so I can act like an artist." Someone else was added.

Is it, however, dishonest?

The first AI-generated art competition success has surely inflamed the debate around AI art. Although there are distinctions, some people relate it to the day Deep Blue defeated Gary Kasparov in a chess match. AI art generators take their cues from the prompt writer rather than making their own judgments. Many of those who use AI art generators to create art are artists, but many others are not. There will surely be debate about whether or not an AI prompt writer is an artist.

One could argue that an AI art generator, like a paintbrush, is a tool. So, is it immoral to use an AI art generator to create art for a competition? As the world's largest community-driven AI art marketplace, we believe it is not unethical and urge Colorado State Fair organisers to include an AI art category the following year.

They must be thinking about it. Understand more about AI art to improve your chances of winning.
